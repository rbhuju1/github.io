This is a photograph of Susise Whitlock Moten with her son, Edewin Donerson Moten Jr. This is the only and youngest son of Susise and Dr. E. Moten. She was born in 1881, and worked as a public school teacher. Dr. Edwin. Moten was born in 1877, he worked there as a physician, and had his own office on 702 Bell Ave. They lived in Quakertown on 79 Holt (Vine) St. from 1910-192(2), and had E.D in 1920. In the photograph Susise looks to the side while her son looks directly at the camera. Both of them are sitting down on a lawn with a fence surrounding them. The background is next to a bush and side of a house on the left. This is a very candid image of the mother and son, not including the other two daughters Carrie Annetta Moten born in 1908 the oldest, Myrtle Bell Moten born less than a year late in 1909 or Dr. Moten in the frame. His maternal grandmother also lived with them at the time Caroline Witlock. Image Caption: Susie Whitlock Moten with E. D. These are the two daughters of Susise and Dr. E. Moten.Carrie Annetta Moten born in 1908 the oldest, Myrtle Bell Moten born less than a year late in 1909. Their younger brother E.D.Moten Jr. is not present because he was born almost 10 years later. The girls lived in Quakertown from 1910-1922ish, on 79 Holt (Vine) St. Much of their early childhood was spent their, with their mother working as a school teacher and their father being a local physician.
