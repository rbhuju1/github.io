Maude Woods Clark Hembrey, who also went by “Maudie” was born in Argyle, Texas, but resided in the Quakertown neighborhood of Denton, Texas between 1905 and 1921. She was the daughter of Minnie Bell Woods and the mother of Reverend William M. Clark. Maude Woods Clark Hembry was the daughter of Sidney & Margaret (Brown) Woods. She married John Amus Clark on Feb 19 1896 in Denton Texas and after his death married Cassendy Ross Hembry, son of Anthony Hembry & Ida Fox. Maude had two children with her first husband, Walter Clark and William McKinley Clark, her only son with Cassendy Ross Hembry was Ross Roscoe Hembry, who died by an accidental gun shot at the age of 9 years old December 10, 1913. C.R. Hembry and Maude Woods- Clark-Hembry owned three propertys in Quakertown, two houses were rented out to other residents. They were given a total of $4,350 for their properties by the city of Denton to relocate.
Maude Woods Clark Hembry House, photograph, circa1905-1921], The Portal to Texas History, UNT LibrariesThis home was built in 1905 at 97 Terry Street in the Quakertown neighborhood of Denton, Texas. It was removed in 1921 to make way for a city park. Horses and railroad ties were used to transport the house to 1129 East Hickory Street in the Solomon Hill neighborhood of Denton, Texas. It was torn down in 2005 and replaced with a modern brick structure. Residents of the house were C. R. (Cassendy Ross) and Maude Hembry, followed in 1962 by William and Alma Clark. Maude and Ross Hembry had a boarding house and restaurant in Quakertown located in Denton County.
