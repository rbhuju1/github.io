A section from the Denton Chronicle about Angeline Burr’s departure from Denton. 1922, The Portal to Texas History
Miss Angeline is the only known departure mentioned in the Denton Record and Chronicle after the people of Quakertown were forced out of their homes. She and her family decided to move to California instead of moving to Solomon Hill like many of her neighbors.
A picture of Angeline Burr’s daughter standing in front of their home: DentonHistory.net, Quakertown Photos
In 1897, Angeline Burr and her four children arrived in Denton from Arkansas. She was the first African-American woman to purchase land in Quakertown, she owned three homes that she rented out. She was known in Quakertown as “Aunt Angeline” and was well loved. She was a nurse and midwife who helped deliver both black and white babies.
